Third Conditional – Introducción

02 / 44

El Third Conditional se usa para hablar de situaciones pasadas hipotéticas que no ocurrieron. Expresa arrepentimiento o crítica sobre el pasado.

IF + past perfect, WOULD HAVE + past participle
SI + pasado perfecto, WOULD HAVE + participio pasado
✅ If I had studied more, I would have passed the exam.
Si hubiera estudiado más, habría aprobado el examen.

✅ If she had left earlier, she wouldn't have missed the train.
Si ella hubiera salido antes, no habría perdido el tren.

✅ If I had known about the party, I would have gone.
Si hubiera sabido lo de la fiesta, habría ido.
📌
Diferencia con First y Second: First = posible, Second = imaginario, Third = pasado hipotético (ya no se puede cambiar).
GRAMÁTICA B1

Third Conditional – Formación

03 / 44
✅ Afirmativo
If I had known, I would have come.
If she had studied, she would have passed.
If we had left earlier, we would have arrived on time.
If he had taken the medicine, he would have felt better.
❌ Negativo
If I hadn't known, I wouldn't have come.
If she hadn't studied, she wouldn't have passed.
If we hadn't left earlier, we wouldn't have arrived on time.
If he hadn't taken the medicine, he wouldn't have felt better.
❓ Pregunta: Would you have gone if you had known about the party?
¿Habrías ido si hubieras sabido lo de la fiesta?
GRAMÁTICA B1

Third Conditional – Usos

04 / 44
😔 Arrepentimiento

Expresar que lamentamos algo que pasó.

If I had studied more, I would have passed.
Si hubiera estudiado más, habría aprobado.
🔍 Crítica

Criticar una acción pasada.

If you had listened to me, you wouldn't have made that mistake.
Si me hubieras escuchado, no habrías cometido ese error.
🤔 Reflexión

Reflexionar sobre cómo pudieron ser las cosas.

If we had taken a taxi, we would have arrived earlier.
Si hubiéramos tomado un taxi, habríamos llegado más temprano.

Verb Patterns – Introducción

06 / 44

Los verb patterns son las estructuras que siguen a ciertos verbos. Algunos verbos requieren -ing, otros to + infinitive, y otros object + infinitive.

📝 Verbo + -ing

Verbos como: like, love, hate, enjoy, finish, stop, avoid

I like swimming.
She enjoys reading.
📝 Verbo + to + infinitive

Verbos como: want, need, hope, plan, decide, promise, would like

I want to travel.
She needs to study.
📝 Verbo + object + infinitive

Verbos como: make, let, want, ask, tell, advise

He made me cry.
I asked her to help.
GRAMÁTICA B1

Verb Patterns – Ejemplos

07 / 44
📝 Verbo + -ing
I like playing football.
She hates getting up early.
We enjoy watching movies.
He finished eating dinner.
They avoid eating meat.
📝 Verbo + to + infinitive
I want to travel to Europe.
She needs to study more.
We hope to see you soon.
He decided to stay home.
They promised to help.
💡 Diferencia clave:
Stop + -ing = dejar de hacer algo
Stop + to = parar para hacer algo
✅ I stopped smoking. (Dejé de fumar.)
✅ I stopped to smoke. (Paré para fumar.)
GRAMÁTICA B1

Verb Patterns – MAKE y LET

08 / 44

MAKE y LET tienen una estructura especial: verbo + objeto + infinitive sin "to".

🔨 MAKE (obligar / hacer que)
My mom made me clean my room.
Mi mamá me hizo limpiar mi habitación.

The movie made me cry.
La película me hizo llorar.

He made her laugh.
Él la hizo reír.
🔓 LET (permitir)
My parents let me go to the party.
Mis padres me dejaron ir a la fiesta.

Please let me explain.
Por favor, déjame explicar.

She let him drive her car.
Ella le permitió conducir su auto.
📌
¡MAKE y LET no usan "to" después del objeto! Make me cry (no "to cry").

Question Tags – Introducción

09 / 44

Las question tags son pequeñas preguntas al final de una oración para confirmar información o buscar acuerdo.

Oración afirmativa + tag negativa
Oración negativa + tag afirmativa
✅ You're a student, aren't you?
Eres estudiante, ¿verdad?

✅ She can swim, can't she?
Ella sabe nadar, ¿verdad?

✅ He doesn't like coffee, does he?
A él no le gusta el café, ¿verdad?
📌
Regla: Si la oración es afirmativa, la tag es negativa. Si la oración es negativa, la tag es afirmativa.

As...as – Comparaciones de igualdad

12 / 44

AS...AS se usa para comparar dos cosas que son iguales en algún aspecto.

As + adjetivo/adverbio + as
✅ She is as tall as her sister.
Ella es tan alta como su hermana.

✅ He runs as fast as me.
Él corre tan rápido como yo.

✅ This book is as interesting as that one.
Este libro es tan interesante como ese.
❌ Negativo: not as...as (no tan...como)
She is not as tall as her sister.
Ella no es tan alta como su hermana.

Giving Advice – Introducción

19 / 44

Para dar consejos en inglés, usa expresiones como should, ought to, why don't you, if I were you.

💡 Expresiones para dar consejos
You should study more.
Deberías estudiar más.

You ought to apologize.
Deberías disculparte.

Why don't you talk to her?
¿Por qué no hablas con ella?

If I were you, I would accept the offer.
Si fuera tú, aceptaría la oferta.
⚠️ Advertencias
Be careful! The floor is wet.
¡Ten cuidado! El piso está mojado.

Watch out! There's a car coming.
¡Cuidado! Viene un auto.

Don't touch that! It's hot.
¡No toques eso! Está caliente.

Giving Advice – Diálogo

20 / 44
