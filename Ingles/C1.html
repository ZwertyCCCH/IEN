GRAMÁTICA A1

Verb To Be — Forma Afirmativa

02 / 20

El verbo "to be" (ser/estar) es el verbo más importante del inglés. Se usa para describir identidad, profesión, características, estados y ubicaciones.

1st singularI amI'm
2nd singularYou areYou're
3rd singular ♂He isHe's
3rd singular ♀She isShe's
3rd singular (thing)It isIt's
1st pluralWe areWe're
3rd pluralThey areThey're
✅ I'm a student at the Naval School.
✅ She's a doctor. He's tall.
✅ We're from Chile. They're officers.
✅ It's cold today. The ship is big.
💡
Regla de uso: Se usa TO BE para: nombre (I'm Carlos), edad (He's 20), profesión (She's a nurse), descripción (It's fast), origen (We're Chilean), estado (I'm tired), ubicación (They're in Valparaíso).

Verb To Be — Negativo e Interrogativo

03 / 20
Forma Negativa
I am not → I'm not
He/She/It is not → isn't
You/We/They are not → aren't
✅ I'm not tired.
✅ She isn't at home.
✅ They aren't students.
❌ I amn't (incorrecto)
❌ He's not a doctor?
Forma Interrogativa — sujeto al final
Am I ...?
Is he / she / it ...?
Are you / we / they ...?
❓ Are you Chilean? — Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
❓ Is she a pilot? — Yes, she is.
❓ Is it cold? — No, it isn't.
Respuestas cortas — Short Answers
Are you a student? → Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
Is he tall? → Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.

Articles: A / AN / THE

05 / 20
A — artículo indefinido

Antes de consonante.
Referencia no específica.

a book
a cat
a ship
a house*
AN — artículo indefinido

Antes de sonido vocálico.
(a, e, i, o, u)

an apple
an egg
an ice cream
an hour*
THE — artículo definido

Cosa específica/ya mencionada.
Única en el mundo.

the sun / the moon
the Pacific Ocean
the Esmeralda
the president
✅ I have a dog. The dog is black.
✅ She is an officer in the Chilean Navy.
✅ I eat an orange. The orange is sweet.
💡
*Excepción sonido H: "a hotel" (h suena) / "an hour" (h muda, suena /aʊər/). La regla es por sonido, no por letra.
Sin artículo (Zero Article)

Con nombres propios, países, idiomas, materias: I speak English. She is from Chile. I love music.

06 / 20

Los demostrativos indican distancia (cerca / lejos) y número (singular / plural).

Singular (uno)Plural (varios)
Cerca (here)this — este/estathese — estos/estas
Lejos (there)that — ese/aquelthose — esos/aquellos
Como adjetivo (+ sustantivo)
This book is interesting.
That ship is very big.
These shoes are new.
Those buildings are old.
Como pronombre (solo)
This is my uniform.
That is the captain.
These are my friends.
Those are Chilean flags.
📌
Al teléfono: "Hello, this is Carlos." — Al señalar algo lejano: "What's that?"

Present Simple Tense

07 / 20

Se usa para rutinas, hechos y verdades generales. Con he/she/it se añade -s / -es / -ies.

AFIRMATIVO: Subject + verb (base)
He/She/It + verb + s/es

NEGATIVO: Subject + do/does not + verb
PREGUNTA: Do/Does + Subject + verb?
Reglas de la -S para He/She/It
Mayoría → +s
work→ works
read→ reads
eat→ eats
-sh/-ch/-o/-x/-s → +es
watch→ watches
go→ goes
wash→ washes
consonante+y → -y+ies
study→ studies
fly→ flies
carry→ carries
✅ I study English every day.
✅ She watches the news at night.
✅ He doesn't like coffee.
❓ Does she work in the Navy? — Yes, she does.
⏰
Marcadores temporales: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day/week/year, on Mondays, at 8 am.

Ask & Give Instructions — Imperatives

16 / 20

Las instrucciones usan el imperativo: verbo en forma base, sin sujeto. Para hacerlo negativo: Don't + verb.

Afirmativo: Verb + complement → Open the door.
Negativo: Don't + verb → Don't run inside.
Cortés: Please + verb / Verb + please → Please sit down. / Sit down, please.

Expressing Personal Preferences

17 / 20

Para expresar gustos y preferencias se usan estructuras con like, love, hate, prefer + sustantivo o verbo en -ing.

I like / love / enjoy / prefer + noun or verb-ing
I don't like / hate / can't stand + noun or verb-ing
like swimming / love music / hate waking up early
Escala de preferencia
❤️ I love football.
😊 I like swimming.
😐 I don't mind cooking.
😕 I don't like fish.
😡 I hate getting up early.
Preguntas y respuestas
A:Do you like English?
B:Yes, I love it! It's very interesting.
A:What do you like doing in your free time?
B:I like reading and playing football.
A:Do you prefer tea or coffee?
B:I prefer coffee. I don't like tea very much.
📌
Recuerda: después de like/love/hate/enjoy se usa el verbo en -ing: "I like swimming" ✅ — "I like swim" ❌

Vocabulary in Context — Ejemplos tipo examen

19 / 20

El examen evalúa vocabulario en contexto: debes elegir la palabra correcta según el significado de la oración. Aquí hay ejemplos del tipo de preguntas A1.

Tipo 1 — Completar la oración
My ______ is a doctor. She works at the hospital.
a) father   b) mother   c) brother   d) son

The Chilean flag is red, white and ______.
a) orange   b) yellow   c) blue   d) purple
Tipo 2 — Verbo correcto (presente simple)
Carlos ______ English every day after school.
a) study   b) studies   c) studys   d) is study

They ______ from Chile. They are Peruvian.
a) are   b) aren't   c) isn't   d) is not
Tipo 3 — Artículo correcto
She is ______ officer in the Navy.
a) a   b) an   c) the   d) — (no article)

______ Pacific Ocean is very large.
a) A   b) An   c) The   d) — (no article)

Estrategias para el examen 🎯
  • Lee todo el texto antes de contestar preguntas de comprensión.
  • Identifica el contexto para preguntas de vocabulario — la oración da pistas.
  • Para gramática: identifica el sujeto y elige la forma verbal correcta.
  • Para artículos: escucha el sonido, no solo la letra.
  • En lenguaje funcional: identifica la función del intercambio (sugerir, pedir, agradecer, etc.).
  • Usa el método de eliminación: descarta respuestas claramente incorrectas.
  • No dejes preguntas en blanco — no hay penalización.
Formato del examen

50 preguntas · Selección múltiple (a,b,c,d) · 60 minutos · Hoja de respuestas separada.
