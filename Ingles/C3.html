Possessive Pronouns

02 / 32

Los pronombres posesivos indican pertenencia sin necesidad de repetir el sustantivo. Responden a la pregunta: ¿De quién es?

Adjetivo posesivo + sustantivo
→ This is my book.
Pronombre posesivo (reemplaza todo)
→ This book is mine.
SujetoAdj. PosesivoPron. PosesivoEjemplo
ImymineThis uniform is mine.
YouyouryoursIs this phone yours?
HehishisThe backpack is his.
SheherhersThat idea was hers.
Ititsits *The ship lost its anchor.
WeouroursThe victory is ours!
TheytheirtheirsThose books are theirs.
⚠️
*Its es poco usado como pronombre; normalmente se usa "its" como adjetivo. Para objetos se prefiere reformular: "The car is mine" (no "its" solo).

Prepositions of Time: IN / ON / AT

04 / 32

Las preposiciones de tiempo indican cuándo ocurre algo. Cada una tiene usos específicos.

📅 AT
  • Horas: at 8 am, at 3:30 pm
  • Momentos del día: at noon, at midnight, at night
  • Festividades: at Christmas, at Easter
The exam starts at 9 o'clock.
📅 ON
  • Días: on Monday, on Friday
  • Fechas: on March 15th
  • Días + parte del día: on Sunday morning
  • Festividades con "day": on Christmas Day
We have navigation class on Wednesdays.
📅 IN
  • Meses: in March, in July
  • Años: in 2027, in 1999
  • Estaciones: in winter, in summer
  • Partes del día: in the morning, in the afternoon, in the evening
The ship was built in 2005.

GRAMÁTICA A1 (REVIEW)

Connectors: AND / BUT / BECAUSE

06 / 32

Los conectores unen ideas y mejoran la fluidez del discurso.

➕ AND (y / además)

Añade información similar o secuencial.

I study English and I do physical training.
⚓ The ship is fast and modern.
🔴 BUT (pero)

Contraste u oposición.

I like coffee, but I don't like tea.
⚓ The sea was rough, but the ship sailed safely.
🔵 BECAUSE (porque)

Expresa causa o razón.

I study hard because I want to be an officer.
🎒 I'm tired because I went to bed late.
💡 Ejemplo combinado: I want to join the Navy because I love the sea, but I am nervous about the exam, and I need to practice more.

GRAMÁTICA A2

Past Simple – Regular Verbs (-ED)

09 / 32

Los verbos regulares forman el pasado agregando -ed al final.

AFIRMATIVO: Subject + verb + -ed
NEGATIVO: Subject + did not (didn't) + verb (base form)
PREGUNTA: Did + subject + verb (base form)?
Regla 1: +ed
work → worked
play → played
walk → walked
Regla 2: +d (termina en e)
like → liked
arrive → arrived
change → changed
Regla 3: consonante+y → ied
study → studied
carry → carried
cry → cried
🔊
Pronunciación de -ED: /t/ (walked), /d/ (played), /ɪd/ (wanted). La última se usa con verbos terminados en t o d.

Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Diferencia clave: rutinas vs acciones en progreso.

Present SimplePresent Continuous
UsoHechos, rutinas, verdades generalesAhora mismo, acciones temporales, futuro cercano
Palabras clavealways, usually, often, sometimes, never, every daynow, right now, at the moment, today, this week

Non-Action Verbs (Estado)

18 / 32

Algunos verbos expresan estados, no acciones. Normalmente no se usan en Present Continuous.

🧠 Verbos de pensamiento
believe, know, think (opinión), understand, remember, forget
❤️ Verbos de emoción/gusto
like, love, hate, prefer, need, want
👁️ Verbos de percepción
see, hear, smell, taste, feel (sensación)

Asking for Permission & Making Suggestions

🔑 Asking for Permission (Pedir permiso)
✅ Can I use your phone?
✅ Could I open the window? (más formal)
✅ Is it OK if I leave early?
✅ May I come in, sir? (formal, con autoridad)
Respuestas:

✖ I'm sorry, you can't. / No, please don't.

✅ Let's go to the cinema. (Contracción de Let us)
✅ Why don't we study together?
✅ What about going to the beach?
✅ How about ordering a pizza?
✅ Shall we start the meeting?
Respuestas:
✔ Good idea! / That sounds great. / Yes, let's do that.
✖ I don't think so. / I'd rather not.

Past Simple – Regular vs Irregular

27 / 32
✅ Regular Verbs
+ed / +d / ied
work → worked
play → played
study → studied
stop → stopped

Negativo: didn't + base
Pregunta: Did + sujeto + base?
⚠️ Irregular Verbs
Cambian completamente
go → went
eat → ate
see → saw
buy → bought
think → thought

Negativo/Pregunta: ¡Usan "did" igual que los regulares!
❓ Did you go? (no "did you went")
📌 Consejo clave: En negativas y preguntas, el verbo principal siempre vuelve a su forma base (infinitivo sin to), tanto para verbos regulares como irregulares.
✅ She didn't go to the party. (no "didn't went")
✅ Did you see the ship? (no "did you saw")
